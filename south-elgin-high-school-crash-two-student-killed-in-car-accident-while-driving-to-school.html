Two teenage girls have reportedly passed away tragically on August 31, 2023. This incident happened near South Elgin High School. According to reports, the deceased were among four female passengers who were travelling towards the high school in a Honda Civic. In this post, we will take a look at what actually happened. Keep reading for more details.

South Elgin High School Crash: Two Students Passes Away In Car Accident

On the morning of August 31st, tragedy struck near South Elgin High School when a car accident claimed the lives of two teenage girls. According to ABC Chicago, these young victims were among four female passengers travelling in a Honda Civic en route to the high school.

Around 7 a.m., their vehicle collided with a Mack Semi-Truck, resulting in the unfortunate loss of two girls and injuring the other two. The truck driver also suffered injuries that, thankfully, were not life-threatening. Illinois authorities are currently investigating the collision at South Elgin High School, as reported by CBS. In the aftermath of the accident, emergency responders and other teams were observed diligently clearing the wreckage.

Police Investigate The Causes of South Elgin High School Crash

At this early stage of the investigation, the identities of those involved have not yet been disclosed. On that fateful morning, four teenage girls were heading towards South Elgin High School, as noted by The Daily Herald, with at least two of them being related. As they approached an area just two blocks from the school, their vehicle collided with the semi-truck near Kenyon Road. Following the collision, various first responders rushed to the scene.

William J. Luchsinger, Fire Chief of the South Elgin and Countryside Fire Protection District, shared that his rescue teams had to carefully dismantle the Honda Civic to access the girls inside. After this meticulous effort, they discovered three of the young women unconscious, while sadly, another was pronounced deceased at the scene.

Regrettably, a second victim succumbed to her injuries during treatment. A third girl remains in critical condition, while the fourth sustained non-life-threatening injuries.

In an official statement, Superintendent Suzanne Johnson of School District U-46 expressed how the tight-knit community was deeply affected by the situation. She emphasized that the incident has profoundly shaken the entire community, but it has also triggered a wave of support and assistance from numerous individuals.

“Although our school district is quite sizable, we have managed to maintain that close-knit community atmosphere. It’s astonishing how quickly, in a matter of minutes, this situation unfolded today, leading to an overwhelming display of support,” Johnson remarked.
