When was Jon Rahm born?

Jon Rahm was born in Barrika, Basque Country, Spain, on November 10, 1994. Jon Rahm Rodriguez is his given name. His nationality is Spanish. He is of mixed ancestry. His zodiac sign is Scorpio.

He is the son of Edorta Rahm (father) and Angela Rodriguez (mother) (mother). Eric Rahm is his older brother.

Together with his brother, he was raised in Barrika, Basque Country. He has had a passion for golf since he was a child. His father encouraged him to take up golf.

He started playing collegiate golf with the Arizona State Sun Devils and won 11 college events during his time there. In 2015 and 2016, he became the first golfer to win the Ben Hogan Award twice.

Golf Career:

-As a junior, Jon Rahm finished fifth in the 2015 Pheonix Open. For 60 weeks, he was the top-ranked golfer in the World Amateur Golf Ranking.

-At the 2016 US Open, he finished 23rd as the lowest amateur. He turned professional in golf after the finish of the 2016 US Open.

-His first professional event was the Quickens Loans National, where he finished third. He qualified for the US Open with a third-place finish in that event.

-As a non-member, his runner-up finish in the RBC Canadian Open was enough to earn him a PGA Tour Card for the 2017 season.

-He began 2017 with his first PGA Tour victory, the Farmers Insurance Open Title, which propelled him from 137 to 46 in the Official World Golf Ranking.

Jon was also able to compete in major competitions such as the US Masters, PGA Championship, and World Golf Championship thanks to the title.

-Jon finished two strokes behind winner Dustin Johnson at the World Golf Championship. He lost in the final of his second WGC event, this time to Dustin Johnson.

He climbed to #14 in the world rankings after this event. In his debut Masters, he finished 27th and 4th in the Wells Fargo Championship.

-In the same year, he won the Dubai Duty Irish Open for his first European Tour victory and was named European Tour Rookie of the Year.

Year of 2018, he finished second to Johnson in the Sentry Tournament of Champions for the second year in a row. In April, he won the Open de Espana on the European Tour, and in December, he won a Hero World Challenge tournament with a limited field.

-On April 28, 2019, he and partner Ryan Palmer won the Zurich Classic on the PGA Tour.
