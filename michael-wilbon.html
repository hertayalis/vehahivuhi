Michael Wilbon Biography

Michael Ray Wilbon, popularly known as Michael Wilbon, is an accomplished American commentator who was born and brought up in Chicago, Illinois, United States of America. Currently, he works at The Washington Post as a sportswriter and columnist. Wilbon Michael Career

Michael Wilbon Washington Post

After a summer internship at the newspaper in 1979 and 1980, Wilbon started working for the Washington Post in 1980. He covered college sports, Major League Baseball, the National Football League, and the National Basketball Association before being promoted to a full-time journalist in February 1980. His column in the Post, which dealt with the culture of sport as much as the action on the court.

Wilbon has covered ten Summer and Winter Olympic Games for The Washington Post in his career, every Super Bowl since 1987, almost every Final Four since 1982, and every year since 1987 the NBA Finals.

Wilbon was also the only reporter outside Hawaii to cover the historic basketball upset of top-ranking Virginia by then-NAIA member Chaminade in 1982 (he was in Honolulu covering a college bowl game).

Wilbon earned a name during his time at the Post as one of “the best writer in American newspapers.” In 2001, the Society of Professional Journalists named him the top sports journalist. He has become known more like an ESPN personality in recent years than as a reporter. He wrote his last Washington Post column on December 7, 2010, and officially devoted full-time work to ESPN and ABC.

Michael Wilbon ESPN

Wilbon began co-hosting ESPN’s daily opinion forum Pardon the Interruption (PTI) with Tony Kornheiser on October 22, 2001, after contributing to ESPN’s The Sports Reporters and other cable network shows.

Wilbon was also a member of the NBA Countdown of ABC (hosted by Jalen Rose, Bill Simmons, and Magic Johnson), which was the pre-game show for the NBA telecasts of the network.

At the end of 2006, Wilbon agreed with ESPN to extend a multi-year contract. He offered to resign from the Post after accepting the contract, but chairman Don Graham of the newspaper and editor-in-chief Len Downie both asked him to stay on.

With respect to disputes with his newspaper assignments, the network gained priority therein. The first major conflict took place on February 4, 2007, when instead of Super Bowl XLI, Wilbon covered a Detroit Pistons–Cleveland Cavaliers game.

Michael Wilbon PTI

Tony Kornheiser and Michael Wilbon

Pardon the Interruption (PTI) is a sports television show airing on different ESPN TV channels on weekdays. It is hosted by Tony Kornheiser and Wilbon, who discuss the top stories of the day in “sports and other things” (as Kornheiser put it in the original promo of the show) and often argue about them.

Michael Wilbon Heart Attack

Michael Wilbon First Pitch

During a game of Cubs vs Cardinals at Wrigley Field on August 10, 2008, Wilbon threw out the ceremonial first pitch and then sang as part of the seventh-inning stretch “Take Me Out to the Ballgame.” Wilbon footage wearing a tucked-in Cubs jersey and bouncing the pitch is often shown as a friendly teasing by Kornheiser on Pardon The Interruption.
